Floating Solar

Support system for floating solar panels on water

Introduction

What is this about

The high energy demand and the constant depletion of the fossil fuels led us to shift our focus to renewable energy sources, like the solar energy which is not only the future unlimited source of energy, is also eco-friendly. The major issue with the solar energy production is the requirement of huge spaces of land which is scarcely available in the world and even costly to get. The focus can be diverted to water where there is plenty water surface available. Using solar panels on water bodies effectively reduces the amount of evaporation and at the same time increases the efficiency of solar panels. This project is done as part of my job at Enfrien Innovations LLP, Bangalore. The aim of the project is to develop a system that can hold solar panels on water.

Process

Different stages of development

Stage 1

Understanding the nature of the environment and the constraints of setting a floating solar project in industrial space through Literature survey.

Stage 2

Choosing an appropriate material which satisfies the constraints of using the product in an industrial setting.
Materials of interest: HDPE, LDPE, UPVC, FRP, Aluminium.

Stage 3

Understanding various manufacturing techniques and complexities involved in fabricating a product using specific material.
Processes considered: Blow moulding, Roto moulding, Injection moulding and Extrusion.

Stage 4

Brainstorming ideas and creating designs that would satisfy the above constraints.
Possibilities considered: Single panel module, multiple panel module, fixed angle, variable angle, hollow buoy, pipe design, ballast design.

Stage 5

Running simulations to make corrections for safer design, alongside optimizing the material.
Factor of safety considered: 3-4

Stage 6

Prototyping small scale version of the product and testing with scaled down version of constraints.
Methods considered: 3D printing, prototyping with Aluminium box channels, standard uPVC pipes and brackets.

Stage 7

Understanding the concerns and iterating the design process to create better product.

Stage 8

Finalizing the designs and manufacturing the product in bulk.

Final product

Functionality and the look and feel

The material considered for the final desing is uPVC plastic as it is stronger, water resistant, UV resistant, chemical resistant, has low expansion coefficient, environmental friendly and cost effective. The manufacturing process considered was heat extrusion as it is most viable to manufacture designs with uPVC material. Overall final designs were made cosidering maximum solar panel efficiency and not compromising on manufacturing and setup costs. The designs were made also keeping in mind factors like lattitude and longitude of the location, angle of inclination of solar panels, efficiency of solar panels, cost of production, cost of transportation, cost of assembly and potential profit generation.
